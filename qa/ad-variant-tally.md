### Cross-variant observations

1. **Eyebrow is the systemic gap.** 4 of 5 variants (`class11-hi`, `class12-parent`, `parent-proud`, plus partially `tutorial-dropout`) do not echo the ad's chip text in `variant.eyebrow`. The ad chip is the user's last visual anchor before they hit the landing page — surfacing it as the page eyebrow is a cheap continuity win. Only `class10-curious` does this correctly. Recommended: standardise on "ad chip → variant.eyebrow" as a hard contract for future variants.

2. **`payModal.ctaLabel` is identical across `class12-parent`, `class10-curious`, `parent-proud`, and `tutorial-dropout`** ("Pay ₹99"). Only `class11-hi` localises it ("₹99 bhar de"). Probably intentional — the modal is a high-friction commit step where simplicity outranks voice — but worth flagging as a deliberate decision rather than an oversight.

3. **`payModal.title` and `payModal.subtitle` are likewise near-identical across variants** ("Book the demo class" / "₹99 · A live look ..."). Same reasoning likely applies. If you ever want to A/B test modal commit-rate by voice, these are the levers.

4. **`pricing.scholarshipLine` carries a consistent claim across all variants** — scholarships decided on performance in the demo class, not paperwork. Matches `cohort.ts` `howSteps[1].desc`. Good.

5. **SEO description length is the secondary recurring issue**: `class11-hi` (~187) and `parent-proud` (~184) exceed the 160-char soft cap. Other three are within. Soft warning only.

6. **No variant contradicts `cohort.ts`** on the load-bearing facts: ₹99, ₹19,999, 4 weeks / 4 weekends, 5 students, class 9–12, Sat+Sun, June 13 2026 Saturday, one instructor (not rotating, not plural). This is the highest-severity check and all five pass.
