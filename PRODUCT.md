# PRODUCT.md — Build Space (Logic Labs Cohort 01)

> Source of truth for who Build Space is, who it serves, and how it talks. Distilled from the brand persona document. Read this before designing or writing copy.

## Register

**Brand.** This is a marketing surface — the landing page IS the product impression. Design with a POV; do not collapse into the default tech-SaaS look.

## Product purpose

Build Space runs Logic Labs — a live, 4-week, 5-students-max cohort that teaches Indian Class 9–12 students to build and deploy real AI agents. Cohort 01 starts **June 13, 2026.** Cost: ₹19,999. Format: 8 sessions across 4 weekends, 2 hours each, online, taught by the same active AI practitioner every session.

## Users — two audiences, one page

The page must serve both, but the **parent** is the decider.

### The Anxious Parent (primary visitor — designed for first)

40s. Indian. Engineering or business background. Sees AI everywhere. Has paid for courses before that went nowhere. Wants proof, not promises. Loves their child and is scared of making the wrong call on their behalf.

- **Fear:** Wasting money on another course that goes unused. Child graduating with an outdated degree into a changed world.
- **Wants:** To see their child actually build something. Proof learning happened. Someone credible they can trust with their child's time.
- **Tonal posture:** Warm, credible, patient. Acknowledge the fear first. Answer with proof, not adjectives. Treat them as the intelligent adult they are.

### The Curious Student (secondary visitor — read by parent first, then forwarded)

Class 9–12. Already into coding or robotics. Has used ChatGPT. Tried YouTube tutorials. Got confused and stopped. Slightly intimidated. Waiting for permission to take it seriously.

- **Fear:** Falling behind. Friends getting ahead. Stuck watching tutorials while others are building.
- **Wants:** To build something real. To understand how it actually works. A reason to open the laptop that isn't school.
- **Tonal posture:** Practitioner-to-younger-self. Direct, honest, occasionally blunt, but always rooting for them.

## Brand persona — one sentence

Build Space is a **practitioner who showed up** — an AI engineer in their twenties who looked at what Indian school students are being taught about AI, found it inadequate, and built Logic Labs to do it properly.

## The five traits

1. **Practitioner-first.** Everything taught comes from real work. Not a course on AI. Actual systems built and shipped professionally.
2. **Radically direct.** Says the uncomfortable thing. "Your school CS class won't prepare you." No sugar-coating — honesty as respect.
3. **Genuinely invested.** Built for Indian students specifically. Understands the JEE pressure, the unemployment reality, the stakes.
4. **Quietly confident.** Doesn't shout. Doesn't oversell. Makes a claim and backs it with a challenge.
5. **Fiercely small.** 5 students is not a constraint — it's the product. Scale is treated as the enemy of quality.

## Values

- **Building over consuming.** Convert curiosity into creation. Every session, something is made.
- **Honesty over comfort.** Will tell a student they're not ready. Will tell a parent this isn't exam prep. Will say "non-refundable" without apologising.
- **India-first, always.** JEE syllabus is foundation, not burden. Indian students are not behind — they're undertaught.
- **Intimacy at scale is a lie.** 5 students is the whole point. Refuse to grow in ways that destroy the learning relationship.
- **Outcomes over certificates.** Deployed URL beats PDF certificate every time.

## Enemies (what Build Space is against)

- **Edtech theatre.** "Future-ready skills." "Holistic learning." Courses that produce certificates and no capability.
- **The content dump.** YouTube playlists. 4-hour tutorials. Pre-recorded courses 80% never finish.
- **The Indian talent gap.** 60% of engineering graduates unemployable. Treated as emergency, not statistic.
- **Foreign products dressed for India.** Global edtech with an Indian flag tacked on, called localisation.

## Voice — never say / always say

| Never | Always |
|---|---|
| "Future-ready skills for tomorrow's world." | "By week 4, your child has built and deployed a working AI agent." |
| "We nurture creativity and innovation in young minds." | "They build it. They own it. They can show it to anyone." |
| "Our expert educators bring world-class teaching methodologies." | "The instructor builds AI systems professionally right now — not someone who learned it to teach it." |
| "Join thousands of students on their AI learning journey!" | "5 students. Not thousands. Five. That's the point." |
| "Enrol now and get a free certificate!" | "The first class is free. Come and see. Then decide." |

## Vocabulary

**Use:** build · ship · deploy · real · working · production · agent · practitioner · 5 students · India-first · honest · cohort · JEE maths · yours · demo day.

**Banned:** future-ready · holistic · nurture · empower · innovative · world-class · journey · passionate educators · tomorrow's leaders · digital natives · upskill · ecosystem · unlock potential · synergy · ideate.

**Also banned for this surface:** em dashes (use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, parentheses).

## Tone by situation

- **In the hero / above the fold:** calm, confident, plainly stated. The parent is reading this with their guard up — earn the next scroll.
- **In comparison sections:** direct without being aggressive. State what we do; state what the alternatives do. Let the reader judge.
- **In the FAQ:** real, conversational, honest. If the answer is uncomfortable, say it anyway.
- **In CTAs:** never urgency-bait. "Book the free demo. Decide after." beats "Apply now — 3 spots left!"

## The one sentence that is always true

Build Space exists because Indian school students are not being prepared for the AI revolution, and a practitioner decided that was unacceptable.

## Cohort 01 — the concrete facts

- **Starts:** Saturday, 13 June 2026.
- **Schedule:** Saturday + Sunday, 2 hours per session, 4 weeks = 8 sessions.
- **Format:** Online (browser-only, Chrome or Safari).
- **Batch:** Hard cap of 5 students. Not negotiable, not marketing.
- **Eligible:** Class 9–12, India. Python helpful, not required.
- **Price:** ₹19,999, one-time, UPI. Scholarships based on demo-class performance.
- **Free demo class.** First session is free, includes a live test. Decide after.
- **Outcome:** A deployed AI agent (live URL + GitHub repo), demo day with family invited, certificate.
- **Instructor:** One person, every session. Currently building agentic AI systems in production. Credentials: Recur Club, TCS Bancs, Marxx AI, 5+ years.
- **Parent access:** WhatsApp contact, observer access to any session.

## Anti-references — do not look like these

- Codingal · CuriousJr · Camp K12 · WhiteHat Jr · Scratch-style block coding pages. (Bright cartoon mascots, gradient buttons, "kids coding" stock photography.)
- Generic SaaS landing pages. (Centered hero stack, three icon-title-text cards, "trusted by" logo grid, gradient CTA button.)
- Editorial-magazine-on-tech-brand templates. (Display serif italic headline, mono labels, ruled separators — the Stripe-adjacent Newsreader-italic look.)

## Reference points — do look adjacent to these

- A confident Indian liberal-arts college admissions page (Ashoka, Krea — calm authority).
- Apple's product film aesthetic for the hero video (warm, slow, human).
- A trusted personal letter from a credible educator — not a brochure.
